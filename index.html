Research & Publications

A Quantitative Ultrasound Phantom Calibration Study: Effect of Depth on Attenuation and Backscatter Properties

Salman Jubair Jim; Alex Devlin; Farah Deeba

2024 46th Annual International Conference of the IEEE Engineering in Medicine and Biology Society (EMBC)

This paper examines calibration for Quantitative Ultrasound (QUS). The researchers found that estimation errors (bias) get significantly worse when the attenuation (how much the signal weakens) of the test sample doesn't match the reference phantom. This error increases with the imaging depth (ROI) following a power law. As a solution, the study shows that Envelope SNR deviation can be used to find a reliable calibration depth, even when the sample and reference attenuations are different.

A Framework for Early Diabetes Prediction and Interpretation: Sex-specific Factor Analysis Approach Using Ensemble Classifiers

Irtiza Hasan; Mainul Islam; Mohammad Ehsanul Hoque; Salman Jubair Jim

2024 IEEE International Conference on Biomedical Engineering, Computer and Information Technology for Health (BECITHCON)

Our study proposes a new method for predicting diabetes that uses Exploratory Factor Analysis (EFA) to improve accuracy and make the model easier to interpret. The researchers developed separate models for males and females, finding distinct predictive factors for each sex. By combining EFA with ensemble-based machine learning, their models achieved high accuracy, with F1 scores of 97.62% for males and 98.15% for females. The approach shows that this EFA-driven, sex-specific analysis is highly effective for early diabetes detection.

Non-Invasive Blood Glucose Measurement Using Near Infra-Red Spectroscopy

Anika Tabassum Priyoti; Salman Jubair Jim; Sushmit Hossain; Shafkat Mahmud; Sujana Salvin; Arnab Bhattacharjee

2019 IEEE R10 Humanitarian Technology Conference (R10-HTC)(47129)

This paper presents a non-invasive blood glucose monitor designed to be more accessible and less expensive than traditional invasive methods (needles and strips). The device is worn on a fingertip and uses infra-red (IR) spectroscopy to measure the amount of IR radiation passing through the blood vessels. This measurement is then converted to a glucose concentration (mmol/L) using a calibration curve. A key feature is its method for ensuring accuracy: it uses an IR sensor to measure the distance between the emitter and receiver (compensating for different finger depths) and normalizes the IR absorbance readings to prevent errors.
